# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behaviors

We expect all community members to:

- **Use welcoming and inclusive language**
- **Be respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences**
- **Gracefully accept constructive criticism**
- **Focus on what is best for the community**
- **Show empathy towards other community members**
- **Act with integrity and honesty**
- **Be collaborative and supportive**
- **Listen actively and seek to understand**
- **Give credit where credit is due**
- **Help others learn and grow**

### Unacceptable Behaviors

We do not tolerate:

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, including but not limited to:
  - Unwelcome comments about appearance, body, race, religion, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, age, or other protected characteristics
  - Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
  - Publishing private information without permission
  - Sexually explicit or violent material
  - Any other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

- **Discrimination**: Any form of discrimination based on:
  - Age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity

- **Personal Attacks**: Including but not limited to:
  - Insults, demeaning comments, or personal attacks
  - Trolling, deliberate provocation, or disruption
  - Sustained disruption of discussion or collaboration
  - Publishing private information without explicit permission

- **Unprofessional Conduct**: Including but not limited to:
  - Abusive, harassing, or otherwise unprofessional behavior
  - Inappropriate use of nudity and/or sexual images
  - Threats of violence or incitement of violence
  - Deliberate sabotage or vandalism

## Enforcement Responsibilities

### Community Leaders

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will:

- **Take appropriate and fair corrective action** in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful
- **Provide clear guidelines** for acceptable behavior
- **Act in good faith** to maintain a welcoming and inclusive environment
- **Be transparent** about enforcement decisions
- **Seek input from diverse perspectives** when making enforcement decisions

### Enforcement Process

1. **Report**: Any community member can report violations
2. **Review**: Community leaders will review the report
3. **Investigation**: A thorough investigation will be conducted
4. **Action**: Appropriate action will be taken
5. **Follow-up**: The situation will be monitored

### Consequences

Community leaders may take any of the following actions, in order of severity:

1. **Warning**: Private warning about inappropriate behavior
2. **Temporary Suspension**: Temporary removal from the community
3. **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from the community
4. **Legal Action**: Involvement of law enforcement if necessary

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include:

- **Using an official email address**

- **Making presentations on behalf of the community**

- **Forums and discussion boards**: Community forums
- **Chat platforms**: Slack, Discord, and other chat platforms
- **Email lists**: Community mailing lists

### Offline Spaces

- **Meetups and conferences**: In-person events
- **Workshops and trainings**: Educational events
- **Hackathons and competitions**: Community events
- **Social gatherings**: Community social events

### What to Include in Your Report

Please include the following information in your report:

1. **Your contact information** (optional, for follow-up)
2. **The date and time** of the incident
3. **The location** where the incident occurred
4. **The people involved** (if known)
5. **A detailed description** of what happened
6. **Any evidence** you have (screenshots, logs, etc.)
7. **The impact** it had on you or others

### Confidentiality

- **Privacy**: Your report will be kept confidential
- **Anonymity**: You can report anonymously if preferred
- **Protection**: We will protect you from retaliation
- **Support**: We will provide support throughout the process

### Response Timeline

- **Acknowledgment**: Within 24 hours
- **Initial Assessment**: Within 48 hours
- **Investigation**: Within 5 business days
- **Resolution**: As soon as possible
- **Follow-up**: Within 30 days

## Conflict Resolution

### Informal Resolution

For minor conflicts, we encourage:

- **Direct Communication**: Speak directly with the person
- **Mediation**: Ask a community leader to mediate
- **Education**: Provide education on community standards
- **Clarification**: Clarify misunderstandings

### Formal Resolution

For serious conflicts, we will:

- **Investigation**: Conduct a thorough investigation
- **Documentation**: Document all findings
- **Decision**: Make a fair and impartial decision
- **Action**: Take appropriate action
- **Appeal**: Allow for appeals if necessary

## Enforcement Examples

### Example 1: Inappropriate Language

**Situation**: A community member uses offensive language in a GitHub issue.

**Response**:

1. **Private Warning**: Community leader sends a private warning
2. **Education**: Provide education on appropriate language
3. **Monitoring**: Monitor future behavior
4. **Escalation**: Escalate if behavior continues

### Example 2: Harassment

**Situation**: A community member repeatedly sends unwanted messages to another member.

**Response**:

1. **Immediate Action**: Immediate temporary suspension
2. **Investigation**: Thorough investigation of the incident
3. **Decision**: Permanent ban if harassment is confirmed
4. **Support**: Provide support to the victim

### Example 3: Constructive Feedback

**Situation**: A community member provides constructive but critical feedback.

**Response**:

1. **Appreciation**: Thank the member for their feedback
2. **Discussion**: Discuss the feedback constructively
3. **Action**: Take appropriate action based on the feedback
4. **Follow-up**: Follow up on the changes made

## Appeals Process

### Right to Appeal

Any community member has the right to appeal enforcement decisions.

### Appeal Process

1. **Submit Appeal**: Submit appeal to appeals@samiul.devops-portfolio.com
2. **Review**: A different community leader will review the appeal
3. **Decision**: A final decision will be made
4. **Communication**: The decision will be communicated to all parties

### Appeal Timeline

- **Submission**: Submit appeal within 7 days of original decision
- **Review**: Review within 5 business days
- **Decision**: Final decision within 10 business days
- **Implementation**: Implement decision immediately
